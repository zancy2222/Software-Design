Lesson Summary

Overview of Emerging Technologies

Emerging technologies are novel, fast-growing, and transformative, significantly impacting business and society. Key examples include:

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI): Evolving since the 1950s, AI has applications in various sectors like healthcare and finance.
  • Augmented Reality (AR): Enhances real-world views with computer-generated elements, used in gaming, retail, and automotive.
  • Quantum Computers: Utilize quantum mechanics for complex calculations, potentially revolutionizing fields such as material design and molecular simulation.

The Fourth Industrial Revolution (Industry 4.0)

Industry 4.0 integrates advanced technologies into manufacturing, transforming production and lifestyle. It follows three prior industrial revolutions and incorporates IoT, robotics, VR, AR, AI, Big Data Analytics, and Cloud Computing, enhancing efficiency and connectivity.

Requirements Analysis and Modeling

This phase defines user requirements and converts them into detailed system requirements through:

  • Gathering Requirements
  • Analyzing Requirements
  • Modeling User Needs

Requirements can be functional or non-functional, influencing the system's design and performance.

Design Principles and Patterns

Good design principles, like the SOLID principles, help create flexible and maintainable software architectures. Key principles include:

  • Single Responsibility Principle (SRP)
  • Open-Closed Principle (OCP)
  • Liskov Substitution Principle (LSP)
  • Interface Segregation Principle (ISP)
  • Dependency Inversion Principle (DIP)

Design patterns provide reusable solutions to common design problems, categorized into creational, structural, and behavioral patterns.

Prototyping and Quality Assurance

Prototyping involves building preliminary versions of systems to clarify requirements and improve design. Software Quality Assurance (SQA) ensures that software meets requirements and is fit for use, focusing on defect prevention, detection, and removal.

Software Testing and Deployment

Software testing verifies that systems function correctly under specified conditions, aiming to detect defects and ensure requirements are met. Types of testing include:

  • Unit Testing
  • Integration Testing
  • System Testing
  • Performance Testing
  • Acceptance Testing
